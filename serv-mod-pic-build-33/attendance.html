Long-Term Care

Modernising Our Services

Jake is a Retirement Pension claimant who has experienced difficulties with remembering things and making routine decisions recently.

Jake and his son Paul attend a doctor’s appointment, where they learn that Jake’s symptoms are associated with dementia, and that they are likely to deterioriate further.

The doctor provides Jake and Paul with information about what to do next, and where they can find further support. Paul intends to fund his father's care himself.

A cross-governmental approach to signposting will save the citizen's time and effort when engaging with any government service.

After some time, Paul is unable to continue self-funding the costs of his father’s care. Paul goes online to GOV.UK to find what support he might be able to get.

As Jake struggles to remember things, it would be difficult for him to manage his own claim. Jake’s son Paul registers to become his appointee, so that he can easily manage his father’s affairs across DWP services.

We're making it easier to engage with our services, whether it's online, over the telephone, or you're claiming for someone else as a formal appointee.

Now that Paul acts as his father’s appointee, he can apply for Attendance Allowance for his father’s care. Paul fills in the claim online, and registers for notifications to come through to his preferred channel.

We can confirm evidence provided on claims by engaging with external organisations such as the NHS and by checking previous claims to benefit.

Because it’s easier for us to confirm the evidence that has been provided, we can provide an expedient decision.

Streamlined, automated decisions lead to claims being processed faster, and citizens getting their first payment sooner. Accurate payments help citizens pay for essential services in urgent and difficult situations.

Now that his father’s care is funded through Attendance Allowance, Paul can easily manage the claim online. Changes in costs, payment details and any other change in circumstances can be reported via his preferred channel.

Appointees have to juggle managing someone else’s affairs as well as their own. Self-service can give appointees the flexibility to manage in their own time.
