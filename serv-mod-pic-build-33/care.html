Caring for Others

Modernising Our Services

Saima has been caring for her disabled mother, Huma for some time. Saima’s mother receives PIP for her condition, however, Saima is beginning to find it difficult to balance caring for her mother, while working sufficient hours to meet their household bills.

Saima routinely helps Huma with the letters she receives. While reading a DWP letter addressed to Huma, Saima starts to wonder if the DWP have available support for people in her situation, and decides to visit the website address on the letter, GOV.UK.

GOV.UK will be even easier to navigate. We're simplifying the language, and enabling citizens so they have straight forward routes through to our services, guidance and the support we offer.

Saima visits GOV.UK, and is presented with clear, accurate guidance, as well as tools that will make it easy for her to find out what she's entitled to.  After reading through the guidance and eligibility information, Saima learns she may be entitled to Carer's Allowance. Saima can start her application immediately online.

Saima’s commitments would make it difficult for her to call during operating hours. Being able to self-serve online at her preferred time suits her needs, as well as reduces demand on our telephony networks.

The DWP already know that Saima and her mother live together, and that Saim’s mother has a PIP claim in payment.

We’re able to effectively identify Saima using our robust identity verification technology, as well as utilize information we already hold, which will streamline the citizen experience.

We can confirm the claim faster because of the information we already hold, as well as our knowledge of customer circumstances. We're automating how we check citizen information, such as earnings, employment, residency and presence.

Payments will be frictionless, faster, and more transparent. We'll make it easier for citizens to manage their financial information.

The DWP provide guidance to Saima on how to manage her claim and report a change of circumstances, as well as when she will receive her first payment.

After some time, Huma experiences an injury which means she'll have to spend some time in hospital. Saima contacts the DWP to tell us about the change to her caring responsibilities.

Saima can report a change of circumstances through multiple channels, including self-service, making it easier for her to manage her claim during difficult times.

When we have the relevant information, we'll calculate breaks in care automatically, reducing the need for manual calculations and freeing up skilled agents to help those with more complex needs.

The DWP notify Saima and explain how this will affect her payments in the future. Now that Saima has reported her change online, we can take the neccessary steps to update her claim.

After Huma returns from her hospital stay, Saima notifies the DWP that she is returning to her caring responsibilities. Because Saima is receiving additional financial support, she can reduce her hours at work, and spend more quality time with her mother.
